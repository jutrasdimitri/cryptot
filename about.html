BITCOIN — VIRES IN NUMERIS

Peer-to-Peer Money. Fixed Supply. Absolute Scarcity.

Whitepaper

Bitcoin was created to solve one core problem:

money that can be inflated, censored, or controlled by intermediaries.

At its heart, Bitcoin is peer-to-peer electronic cash — a system that allows anyone, anywhere in the world, to send value without trusting a bank, a government, or a third party.

The Core Idea

21 million coins. Forever.

No central issuer.

No money printing.

No bailouts.

Inflation is not an accident — it is a feature of the traditional banking system.

Banks create money out of debt, silently eroding purchasing power over time.

Bitcoin fixes this.

Because Bitcoin cannot be printed, it becomes a neutral, global medium of exchange and a long-term store of value.

Scarcity is not marketing — it is enforced by code.

The Byzantine Problem

Bitcoin solved the Byzantine Generals Problem:

how strangers can agree on truth without trusting each other.

Through Proof of Work and decentralized consensus, Bitcoin created:

Immutable history

Unforgeable cost

Absolute finality

For the first time in history, digital scarcity existed.

Satoshi Nakamoto

Bitcoin was introduced in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto, an anonymous founder who disappeared after launching the network.

No leader.

No CEO.

No foundation.

Bitcoin belongs to no one — and therefore, to everyone.

Timeline

2008 — Bitcoin whitepaper released

2009 — Genesis block mined

2010 — First real-world transaction (10,000 BTC for pizza)

2012–2020 — Adoption, halvings, network hardening

2021–2025 — Institutional adoption, ETFs, nation-state interest

What Bitcoin Is

Digital gold

Final settlement layer

Monetary base for the future

Bitcoin is not here to replace Visa.

Bitcoin is here to replace central banks.

Final Thought

Wake up.

Bitcoin’s supply is fixed.

Global demand is not.

Every currency will eventually be priced in Bitcoin.

ETHEREUM — CODE IS LAW

Bitcoin was money. But money wasn’t enough.

Whitepaper

Bitcoin was money. But money wasn’t enough.

Bitcoin proved that decentralized value transfer was possible.

Ethereum asked the next question:

What if blockchains could run programs?

The Core Idea

Ethereum is a global, decentralized computer.

It allows developers to deploy smart contracts — unstoppable programs that run exactly as written, without intermediaries.

This enables:

DeFi (banks without banks)

NFTs (digital ownership)

DAOs (organizations without CEOs)

Why Ethereum Exists

Bitcoin is optimized for security and scarcity.

Ethereum is optimized for programmability.

Ethereum didn’t replace Bitcoin — it extended the blockchain idea.

Vitalik Buterin

Ethereum was proposed in 2013 by Vitalik Buterin, a young developer who saw that Bitcoin’s scripting language was intentionally limited.

Vitalik envisioned a general-purpose blockchain.

The result was Ethereum.

The Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM)

The EVM allows any developer to deploy code that:

Cannot be censored

Cannot be altered

Cannot be stopped

This is why Ethereum became the backbone of Web3.

Timeline

2015 — Ethereum launches

2017 — ICO boom

2020 — DeFi explosion

2022 — Ethereum transitions to Proof of Stake

2023–2025 — Layer 2 scaling, global adoption

What Ethereum Replaces

Traditional finance infrastructure

Middlemen

Trusted third parties

Ethereum is not a company.

It is infrastructure.

Final Thought

Bitcoin is digital gold.

Ethereum is digital law.

Why Cardano Exists
